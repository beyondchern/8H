Still sitting with a laptop. Just wondering about the difference between ‘a time’ and ‘the time’ in our life. Which moment is significant enough to be given ‘the’? Which moment is buried and forgotten amongst many many other small moments? If I am correct, my grandmother is currently 98 and was born also in 1922.

For almost two months, I have been sharing a 13 square meters room with another person. In this one room, we work, sleep, exercise, cook, and eat. On a single, A0 size table, we work and dine. All the everyday events happen in one shared room means that before and after any action, there are space transformations: things are stored away (laptops, bowls from the table; mattress, standing lamp, stove from the floor); table/ floor is cleaned up; the things for the coming events are taken out and placed, and after the event, things are stored away again. The transition from one activity to another becomes an event in itself. The need to transform spaces for another event to take place also means there needs to be more or less a wrap up in every action-in-progress. When there is not enough space, we create space in the form of time slots – spaces in the flow. Therefore, there’s no room for “go away” from what you are doing and “come back” to it later.
